# Author Portraits

## Paul Graham
#2000s

Paul Graham is a computer scientist, entrepreneur, and influential essayist who co-founded Y Combinator in 2005, the prestigious startup accelerator that funded companies like Airbnb, Dropbox, and Reddit. Technology journalist Steven Levy describes him as a "hacker philosopher" whose essays on entrepreneurship, programming, and innovation have shaped how founders think about building companies. His writing approach of "thinking out loud" and his widely-read essays on paulgraham.com have made him one of the most influential voices in the startup world.

## Rolf Hichert
#2000s

From 2004, Hichert dedicated himself to developing standardized approaches to business communication, believing that business reports should be as universally understandable as musical scores or circuit diagrams. In 2008, he published the SUCCESS Rules, which later evolved into the International Business Communication Standards in 2013. Today, he serves as president of the IBCS Association and is recognized as one of the leading luminaries in data visualization, particularly in business communication.

---

## Barbara Minto
#1970s

Barbara Minto became the first female MBA hired by McKinsey & Company in 1963, where she quickly distinguished herself through exceptional writing skills and developed the revolutionary Pyramid Principle while editing reports. After leaving McKinsey following the 1973 Oil Crisis, she established her own training business and published "The Pyramid Principle" in 1985, which became fundamental to business communication worldwide. She is also the originator of the MECE principle (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive), now essential to structured business thinking across consulting and corporate strategy.

## Lee Robinson
#2020s

Lee Robinson is a developer, writer, and educator currently working at Cursor teaching about AI, previously spending years at Vercel where he became known for his clear, practical approach to explaining complex developer concepts. His essays on developer experience, documentation, and community building combine technical depth with conversational accessibility, making advanced concepts approachable for developers at all levels. With 15 years of coding experience, he represents a new wave of technical writers who bridge engineering excellence with accessible teaching, actively creating content across multiple platforms while engaging in advisory roles and angel investing.

---

## The Economist
#1980s

Founded in 1843, The Economist has become globally renowned for its analytical rigor, clarity of writing, and distinctive house style that publishes articles anonymously to emphasize collective wisdom over individual bylines. The Economist Style Guide, first published in 1986, has influenced generations of journalists and business writers with its emphasis on precision, brevity, and clarity, becoming essential reading for business leaders, policymakers, and educated readers worldwide. Its approach to communication—combining data-driven analysis with accessible prose—has shaped how professionals think about clear, effective business writing far beyond journalism.

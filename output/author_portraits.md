# Author Portraits

## Paul Graham
#2000s

Paul Graham was born in England in 1964, combining philosophy, computer science, and painting studies. After selling Viaweb to Yahoo, he co-founded Y Combinator in 2005, revolutionizing startup funding. His essays on paulgraham.com became Silicon Valley required reading—a "hacker philosopher" bridging technical rigor with humanistic thinking.

---

## Rolf Hichert
#2000s

Rolf Hichert followed a path from mechanical engineering through academia to consulting, discovering his passion for professional visualization at McKinsey. He dedicated himself to making business communication universally understandable, co-founding Hichert+Partner and publishing the SUCCESS Rules (2008). In 2013, he transferred his IP to the IBCS Association under Creative Commons.

---

## Barbara Minto
#1970s

Barbara Minto boldly applied to Harvard Business School without an undergraduate degree, graduating in 1963 as one of eight women in 600 men. McKinsey hired her as their first female MBA consultant, where she developed the Pyramid Principle and MECE framework. After 1973 layoffs, she built her own training business.

---

## Lee Robinson
#2020s

Lee Robinson is a developer, writer, and educator currently at Cursor, previously at Vercel. His essays on developer experience combine technical depth with conversational accessibility. With 15 years experience, he represents technical writers who bridge engineering excellence with accessible teaching.

---

## The Economist
#1980s

Founded in 1843, The Economist became globally renowned for analytical rigor, clarity, and its distinctive anonymous house style. The Economist Style Guide (1986) influenced generations of business writers with its emphasis on precision, brevity, and clarity.

---

## Stephen King
#1970s

Stephen King grew up in difficult circumstances, writing constantly while teaching high school. Doubleday accepted "Carrie" in 1973, launching his full-time writing career at age 26. Over five decades and 50+ books, he remains grounded—still writing daily with the discipline of someone who never forgot scraping by.

---

## Heinrich von Kleist
#1800s

Heinrich von Kleist (1777-1811) war ein rastloser Geist, getrieben von dämonischer Genialität. Kants Schriften erschütterten ihn existenziell; Goethes Ablehnung traf ihn tief. Am 21. November 1811 erschoss er am Kleinen Wannsee Henriette Vogel und sich selbst—ein verzweifelter letzter Akt.

---

## Daniel Miessler
#2020s

Daniel Miessler spent his career in information security before going independent six months before ChatGPT launched. His Unix-philosophy approach to security assessment translated perfectly to building AI systems, demonstrating that system design matters more than model intelligence. He focuses on "Human 3.0"—upgrading humans for a post-labor economy through personal augmentation.

---

## Alex Albert
#2020s

Alex Albert joined Anthropic as one of its first prompt engineers, later becoming Developer Relations lead. He's known for practical, pragmatic prompt engineering—recognizing that clarity matters more than tricks and that messy user reality rarely matches clean eval cases.
